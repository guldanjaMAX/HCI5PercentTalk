## PART 1: EXISTING FRAMEWORKS & BEST PRACTICES RESEARCH

### A. Existing Conflict Assessment Tools

#### 1. Thomas-Kilmann Conflict Mode Instrument (TKI)
- **Framework:** Measures behavior along two dimensions—assertiveness and cooperativeness
- **Five Conflict Styles:** Competing, Collaborating, Compromising, Avoiding, Accommodating
- **Since:** 1970s, gold standard for 40+ years
- **Usage:** HR/OD consultants, all organizational levels, facilitates discussions on difficult issues
- **Strength:** Proven diagnostic tool for understanding conflict-handling modes
- **Limitation:** Individual-focused, not organizational-readiness focused

**Source:** [Thomas-Kilmann Conflict Mode Instrument](https://www.themyersbriggs.com/en-US/Products-and-Services/TKI)

#### 2. Organizational Readiness for Change Framework (ORCA/TORC)
- **Key Dimensions:** Strength of evidence, organizational context, implementation capacity
- **Change Components:** Change efficacy, change commitment, change valence, informational assessment
- **Context Factors:** Culture/climate, policies, organizational support, trust, past experience
- **Application:** Identifies barriers and facilitators, prioritizes implementation sites
- **Value for HCI:** Provides precedent for multi-dimensional organizational assessment

**Sources:** [Organizational Readiness Assessment](https://www.nccmt.ca/resources/search/187), [Readiness Systematic Review](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s12913-020-4926-z)

#### 3. Organizational Conflict Communication Instrument (OCCI)
- **Styles:** Control, Solution Orientation, Non-Confrontation
- **Application:** Scholarly standard for categorizing conflict resolution styles
- **Data:** Research measures how communication patterns predict conflict outcomes

**Source:** [Conflict Management Styles Analysis](https://www.aasmr.org/jmcde/Vol.2/Vol.2.No.1.04.pdf)

#### 4. Glasl's Nine-Stage Conflict Escalation Model
- **Diagnostic Purpose:** Analyzes conflicts and their development within organizations
- **Intervention Framework:** Different interventions for different escalation stages
  - Stages 1-3: Self-resolution possible
  - Stages 3-5: Professional facilitation needed
  - Stages 5-7: Professional mediation required
  - Stages 7-9: Authority intervention required
- **Application:** Helps organizations identify where they sit on escalation spectrum
- **Value for HCI:** Positions assessment as "where are you vulnerable?" tool

**Sources:** [Glasl's Nine-Stage Model Overview](https://www.toolshero.com/communication-methods/stages-of-conflict-escalation/), [Conflict Escalation Analysis](https://law.uh.edu/blakely/advocacy-survey/Conflict%20Escalation%20Glasl.pdf)

### B. Question Design Best Practices for Lead Gen Assessments

#### Key Principles
1. **Brevity & Accessibility**
   - 10 questions is sweet spot for free diagnostic tools
   - More than 3-4 qualifying questions raises abandonment barrier significantly
   - Each question should take 10-20 seconds to answer

2. **Diagnostic Over Academic**
   - Focus on "What's broken?" not "What's your style?"
   - Problem-oriented questions reveal pain points/gaps
   - Make respondents see what they didn't know they were missing

3. **Mixed Format Approach (RECOMMENDED FOR HCI)**
   - **Primarily Likert 4-5 point scales** (captures nuance better than multiple choice)
   - Avoids "neutral/middle" option that weakens diagnostic value
   - Easier to score and calculate percentile tiers
   - **Why not purely multiple choice:** Forces false binary thinking; less data quality; doesn't capture organizational variation well
   - **Why not purely open-ended:** Difficult to aggregate; slows assessment completion

4. **Smart Segmentation & Logic**
   - Skip logic: Some questions only for certain respondents (e.g., "Do you have formal conflict resolution process?" Yes→follow-up questions; No→skip to different branch)
   - Tailors experience and reduces fatigue

5. **Question Construction Patterns**
   - Use specific behavioral statements rather than vague concepts
   - Example (Poor): "How would you rate your organization's conflict management?"
   - Example (Better): "When two team members have a conflict, managers know the formal steps to follow"
   - Focuses on observable, measurable gaps

**Sources:** [Lead Gen Quiz Types](https://landerlab.io/blog/lead-generation-quizzes), [Survey Best Practices](https://www.zonkafeedback.com/blog/lead-generation-survey-questions), [Likert vs Multiple Choice](https://www.bryq.com/blog/likert-scale-vs-forced-choice-for-employee-selection)

### C. Scoring & Results Presentation

#### Rubric Best Practices
- **Performance Levels:** Use 4 tiers (even number prevents "gravitating to middle")
- **Analytical vs. Holistic:** Analytical (separate scores per dimension) provides more insight; holistic (single score) simpler but less actionable
- **Benchmarking:** Show how respondent compares to peers/industry standard (creates FOMO/urgency)

#### What Creates the "Aha Moment"
1. **Specificity:** Show them EXACTLY where they're vulnerable, not generic feedback
2. **Comparative Data:** "72% of organizations like yours have formal conflict policies. You don't." (creates urgency)
3. **Cost Translation:** "$359B annual cost nationally = $X in paid hours lost at your company" (makes abstract concrete)
4. **Next Step Clarity:** Assessment flows directly to conversion action (call/email with Megan)

**Sources:** [Rubric Design Best Practices](https://www.nik.edu/citl/resources/guides/instructional-guide/rubrics-for-assessment.shtml), [Assessment Rubrics](https://www.learner.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/02/s7_rubrics.pdf)

### Critical Industry Data for Results Page

#### Conflict Costs
- **$359 billion annually** in paid hours spent on conflict in U.S. (CPP Inc. study)
- **2.8 hours per week** per employee managing conflict
- **Equivalent to 385 million days** annually spent arguing vs. collaborating
- **50% of turnover** is conflict-related (Work Institute 2024)
- **Cost of replacing employee:** 50-200% of annual salary
- **Absenteeism cost:** $3,600 per employee annually in conflict-heavy organizations

#### Manager & Employee Training Gap
- **60% of managers** have never received basic conflict management training
- **Managers spend 20-40% of time** on conflict despite lack of training
- **96% of managers** believe they have good conflict resolution skills
- **Only 27% are rated as "very skilled"** in actual conflict resolution
- **95% effectiveness rate:** Of those who DO receive conflict training, 95% report it helps them navigate conflict positively
- **57% of employees** received some conflict training
- **60% of employees** never received basic conflict management training
- **98% of respondents** agree conflict resolution training is important
- **83% state** it supports role effectiveness

#### Organizational Policy & Process Gap
- **72% of organizations** DO NOT have formal conflict resolution policies
- **Formal processes dramatically improve outcomes:** Organizations with structured escalation, mediation, and clear policies show significantly lower conflict-related turnover

#### Impact on Satisfaction & Retention
- **54% vs 77% satisfaction:** Employees with conflict are significantly less satisfied
- **2x more likely to leave:** Employees with conflict are twice as likely to consider leaving within 12 months (33% vs 16%)

**Sources:** [Workplace Conflict Statistics - Pollack Peacebuilding](https://pollackpeacebuilding.com/workplace-conflict-statistics/), [CPP Cost Study](https://www.inc.com/brian-de-haaff/the-359-billion-problem-driving-your-project-under-the-radar.html), [Workplace Conflict Cost CMOE](https://cmoe.com/blog/workplace-conflict-costs/), [Peaceful Leaders Academy](https://peacefulleadersacademy.com/blog/workplace-conflict-statistics/)

## PART 3: ASSESSMENT DIMENSIONS & QUESTION DESIGN

### Recommended 8 Assessment Dimensions for HCI

**Dimension 1: Leadership Preparedness for Difficult Conversations**
- Does leadership have frameworks for handling high-conflict individuals?
- Can leaders identify early warning signs of high-conflict behavior?
- Are leaders equipped with BIFF-like communication models?
- **Why:** Leadership models behavior; without training, escalation is inevitable

**Dimension 2: Formal Conflict Resolution Processes**
- Does org have documented escalation protocol?
- Is there a clear path from peer conflict → HR → mediation → formal action?
- Are these processes communicated to all employees?
- **Why:** Clarity prevents confusion, escalation, and legal exposure

**Dimension 3: Manager Training & Confidence**
- How many managers have received conflict management training?
- Do managers know when to escalate vs. resolve internally?
- Do managers understand their role in early intervention?
- **Why:** 60% gap in current training; managers spend 20-40% of time on conflict

**Dimension 4: High-Conflict Individual Identification & Management**
- Can organization recognize 4 characteristics of high-conflict personalities (blame, black/white thinking, emotion dysregulation, extreme behavior)?
- Are there protocols specifically for managing these individuals?
- Does organization use strengths-based vs. purely disciplinary approaches?
- **Why:** HCI's core differentiator; most orgs conflate "difficult person" with "high-conflict personality"

**Dimension 5: Communication Culture & Psychological Safety**
- Do employees feel safe raising concerns without retaliation?
- Is there open, honest communication, or politeness hiding problems?
- Are difficult conversations normalized or avoided?
- **Why:** Organizational communication moderates workplace aggression; poor culture exacerbates conflict

**Dimension 6: Escalation Protocol & Intervention Readiness**
- Can organization identify when conflict reaches "professional mediation" stage?
- Are mediators or third-party resources available/trained?
- Is there a playbook for preventing escalation to legal/destructive stages?
- **Why:** Glasl's framework shows interventions must match escalation stage

**Dimension 7: Employee Support Resources & Wellness**
- Are EAP services available for conflict-affected employees?
- Are team dynamics/cohesion actively managed?
- Is there post-conflict repair/team reintegration process?
- **Why:** Conflict-related stress costs $3,600/employee/year in absenteeism; affects retention

**Dimension 8: Policy & Documentation Readiness**
- Does organization have written civility/conduct policies?
- Are expectations for professional behavior clearly defined?
- Are consequences consistently applied/documented?
- **Why:** 72% of orgs lack formal policies; creates legal vulnerability

## PART 4: SCORING RUBRIC & RESULTS TIERS

### Scoring Methodology

**Point Calculation:**
- Sum all 10 question scores (10-40 points total)
- Convert to percentage: (Total Points - 10) / 30 × 100 = 0-100%

**Example:**
- Score 10 = (10-10)/30 × 100 = 0%
- Score 25 = (25-10)/30 × 100 = 50%
- Score 40 = (40-10)/30 × 100 = 100%

## PART 8: COMPETITIVE DIFFERENTIATION

### Why HCI Assessment Is Different

**vs. TKI/Myers-Briggs**
- TKI measures individual conflict styles; HCI measures organizational readiness
- TKI is about how YOU handle conflict; HCI is about whether YOUR ORGANIZATION is prepared
- TKI is personality; HCI is systems + culture + capability

**vs. Generic Conflict Training Companies**
- HCI uniquely focuses on high-conflict personality recognition and management
- Most training is conflict resolution strategies (Thomas-Kilmann, interest-based negotiation)
- HCI adds diagnostic layer: "You have high-conflict personalities in key roles, requiring specialized approach"
- New Ways for Work is proprietary coaching system for managing these individuals

**vs. Organizational Change Consultants**
- Change readiness is about implementing new initiatives
- Conflict readiness is about sustaining culture and leadership through adversity
- HCI combines assessment + training + coaching + frameworks (BIFF, CARS, EAR, New Ways)
- HCI works with individuals, teams, AND systems
