## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The HCI Conflict Readiness Assessment positions HCI differently from all existing conflict tools and frameworks. Rather than measuring individual conflict *styles* (like Thomas-Kilmann), it measures organizational *readiness* and capability—with unique focus on identifying and managing high-conflict *personalities*, not just conflict *situations*.

This positioning creates a clear strategic niche:
- **vs. TKI/Myers-Briggs:** We're not about how you handle conflict; we're about whether your organization is prepared for high-conflict people
- **vs. Generic conflict training:** We identify the personality pattern, then teach specialized management (not standard conflict resolution)
- **vs. Change management readiness:** Conflict readiness is about cultural resilience, not initiative adoption

## THE COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

### 1. Thomas-Kilmann Conflict Mode Instrument (TKI)

**What It Does:**
- Measures 5 individual conflict *styles*: Competing, Collaborating, Compromising, Avoiding, Accommodating
- Assesses respondent's personal preference for handling disputes
- Based on assertiveness vs. cooperativeness dimensions
- Takes 15 minutes, generates personal profile

**Use Case:**
- Team building, leadership development
- Helping individuals understand their conflict behavior
- Facilitating discussions about interpersonal dynamics

**Strengths:**
- Proven 40+ years, gold standard
- Well-researched, valid, reliable
- Accessible, easy to administer
- Generates immediate personal insight

**Limitations:**
- Individual-focused, not organizational
- Doesn't assess organizational readiness
- Doesn't measure policy, process, or systemic capability
- Doesn't specifically address high-conflict personalities
- Can feel generic (not industry or role-specific)

**Price Point:** $50–100 per person (administered through licensing partners)

---

### 2. Generic Conflict Management Training Programs

**What They Do:**
- Teach conflict resolution strategies (interest-based negotiation, principled negotiation, mediation)
- Focus on how to resolve disputes between reasonable people
- Often use "5 Styles" (Thomas-Kilmann based)
- Delivered as 1-2 day workshops

**Examples:**
- Crucial Conversations (Patterson et al.)
- Nonviolent Communication (Rosenberg)
- Interest-Based Relational (IBR) negotiation
- Corporate mediation training

**Strengths:**
- Evidence-based approaches
- Practical, immediately applicable
- Widely available, lots of facilitators
- Good for normal conflict situations

**Limitations:**
- Assume all parties are reasonable and want to resolve conflict
- Don't address high-conflict personalities (who escalate during "resolution" talks)
- Training-only (no assessment, system design, or ongoing coaching)
- One-size-fits-all; not customized to organization
- High failure rate with actually difficult people

**Price Point:** $2K–5K per workshop (vs. $20K–50K for integrated approach)

**Symptom of Gap:** "We trained all our managers in conflict resolution. But we still have trouble with [difficult person]. They don't respond to the techniques we taught."

---

### 3. Organizational Change Readiness Assessments

**What They Do:**
- Measure readiness to implement specific organizational change initiative (new system, restructure, process)
- Assess change efficacy, commitment, resources, culture
- Help prioritize rollout sites or implementation approach
- Developed for healthcare, government implementation frameworks

**Examples:**
- ORCA (Organizational Resource/Context Assessment)
- TORC (Theory of Organizational Readiness for Change)
- Prosci change readiness models

**Use Case:**
- Leading organizational transformation
- Implementing new systems or processes
- Determining site-by-site readiness

**Strengths:**
- Robust, peer-reviewed frameworks
- Validated measurement approaches
- Addresses organizational culture and structure
- Predictive of implementation success

**Limitations:**
- Focused on *initiative* readiness, not *people* readiness
- Assume conflict is resistance to change (not personality-driven)
- Designed for specific change projects, not ongoing readiness
- Expensive consultant-driven process ($50K–200K+ engagement)
- Not accessible as lead-generation tool

**Price Point:** $50K–200K+ for consulting engagement

## HCI'S UNIQUE POSITIONING

### The White Space: Organizational Readiness for HIGH-CONFLICT PEOPLE

**Core Insight:**
Most organizations can manage normal conflict between reasonable people. What they can't manage: *Individuals with high-conflict personalities*.

**The Personality vs. Situation Distinction:**

| Aspect | Normal Conflict | High-Conflict Personality |
|--------|-----------------|--------------------------|
| **Root** | Disagreement about issue | Personality patterns (blame, binary thinking, emotion dysregulation, extremity) |

| **Resolution Approach** | Interest-based negotiation, mediation | Boundaries, consistency, limited engagement, specialized coaching |
| **Manager Frustration** | "We've trained managers but still struggle with Person X" | Person X doesn't respond to normal conflict resolution; it makes it worse |
| **Organizational Cost** | Moderate (lost time, decisions delayed) | Severe (turnover, team departure, legal exposure, culture toxicity) |

**The HCI Difference:**
We don't just teach conflict resolution. We teach *identification* + *specialized management* for high-conflict personalities.
